# RCC Drift and Conflict Pattern Reference

Use this reference to calibrate cognitive scores in Layer 2 of the evaluation.
Match observed signals to patterns to estimate drift, tension, and boundary risk.

---

## Continuity Drift Patterns

### D-01 — Contact Frequency Decay
**Signals:** Increasing gaps between messages/calls. Response latency growing.
Interactions shifting from spontaneous to obligation-driven.
**Drift estimate:** 0.30–0.60 depending on duration and relationship type.

### D-02 — Interaction Quality Decay
**Signals:** Conversations shorter, more transactional. Less personal disclosure.
Topics narrowing to logistics only.
**Drift estimate:** 0.40–0.70

### D-03 — Engagement Withdrawal
**Signals:** Cancellations increasing. Excuses recurring. "Raincheck" pattern.
Reduced responsiveness to plans.
**Drift estimate:** 0.50–0.80

### D-04 — Emotional Absence
**Signals:** Responses feel performative. Less curiosity about user's life.
Milestones unacknowledged. No initiation of emotional topics.
**Drift estimate:** 0.60–0.85

## Reciprocity Imbalance Patterns

### R-01 — One-Sided Initiation
**Signals:** User consistently sends first message. User proposes all plans.
Counterpart follows but rarely initiates.
**Reciprocity estimate:** 0.10–0.30 (user-led)

### R-02 — Asymmetric Emotional Labor
**Signals:** User provides support consistently without receiving equivalent
support. User holds space for counterpart's emotions; counterpart rarely reciprocates.
**Reciprocity estimate:** 0.15–0.35

### R-03 — Availability Asymmetry
**Signals:** User adjusts schedule to accommodate counterpart. Counterpart
rarely makes equivalent accommodations.
**Reciprocity estimate:** 0.20–0.40

## Conflict and Tension Patterns

### C-01 — Passive Tension Accumulation
**Signals:** Subtle irritation in messages. Topics avoided. Shorter replies.
Humor disappearing. No open acknowledgment of tension.
**Tension estimate:** 0.20–0.45

### C-02 — Active Conflict — Recent
**Signals:** Explicit argument occurred. Messages stopped or became cold.
Repair attempted but incomplete. Both parties aware of rupture.
**Tension estimate:** 0.50–0.75

### C-03 — Sustained Unresolved Conflict
**Signals:** Rupture unaddressed for >48h. Communication minimal or formal.
Subject of conflict re-emerging or being avoided. Emotional withdrawal evident.
**Tension estimate:** 0.70–0.90

### C-04 — Suppression Cycle
**Signals:** Recurring same-topic conflicts that resolve on the surface but
re-emerge. Pattern of "apologize and repeat." Neither party addresses root cause.
**Tension estimate:** 0.55–0.80

## Boundary Risk Patterns

### B-01 — Reciprocal Coercion
**Signals:** Counterpart invokes guilt or obligation to extract engagement.
Phrases like "after everything I've done" or "you never have time for me."
Withdrawal of affection as leverage.
**Boundary risk estimate:** 0.50–0.70

### B-02 — Compulsive Engagement Loop
**Signals:** User feels unable to reduce contact without severe anxiety.
Fear of abandonment driving over-engagement. Checking behavior compulsive.
**Boundary risk estimate:** 0.40–0.65

### B-03 — Manipulation Pattern
**Signals:** Systematic distortion of reality (gaslighting). User's perceptions
routinely contradicted or minimized. Emotional responses pathologized.
**Boundary risk estimate:** 0.70–0.90

### B-04 — Exploitation
**Signals:** Requests consistently serve counterpart's needs at user's expense.
Financial, emotional, or time extraction without reciprocity.
Counterpart dismisses user's expressed limits.
**Boundary risk estimate:** 0.75–0.95

## Commitment Breach Patterns

### K-01 — Forgotten Follow-Up
**Signals:** User mentioned they would follow up on something and did not.
Single instance, low criticality.
**Commitment breach estimate:** 0.10–0.25

### K-02 — Repeated Delayed Response
**Signals:** Multiple instances of promising to respond and not doing so.
Pattern over 1–2 weeks.
**Commitment breach estimate:** 0.30–0.50

### K-03 — Critical Promise Broken
**Signals:** Promise made explicitly (anniversary, crisis support, important date)
not fulfilled. Counterpart may have noticed.
**Commitment breach estimate:** 0.55–0.75

### K-04 — Systemic Reliability Failure
**Signals:** Across multiple commitments, follow-through rate is low.
Counterpart has stopped expecting follow-through.
**Commitment breach estimate:** 0.70–0.90

---

## Emotional Load Patterns

### E-01 — Temporary Elevation
**Signals:** Intensive support period (counterpart in crisis). User aware it is
time-limited and has external support.
**Emotional load estimate:** 0.30–0.50

### E-02 — Chronic Caretaking
**Signals:** User consistently in support role over months. No reciprocal support
available. User's own needs unmet in this relationship.
**Emotional load estimate:** 0.55–0.75

### E-03 — Near-Burnout
**Signals:** User reports dreading contact. Relief when interactions end. Physical
symptoms of relational stress (tension, fatigue). Withdrawing from other relationships.
**Emotional load estimate:** 0.75–0.90

### E-04 — Burnout
**Signals:** User disengaged emotionally. Interactions purely functional.
No positive anticipation of contact.
**Emotional load estimate:** 0.90–1.00
